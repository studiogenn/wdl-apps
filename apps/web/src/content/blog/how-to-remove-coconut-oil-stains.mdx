## Does coconut oil stain?

You may be wondering, does coconut oil stain clothes? Are my clothes ruined?

Here’s the short answer up front: It shouldn’t, but it can.

Coconut oil can absorb into whatever you spilled it on and leave a dark spot. Whether your clothes are stained by this oil depends on how long the coconut oil has been sitting on the fabric.

If the coconut oil was only sitting on your clothes for a limited amount of time and was quickly cleaned up, it shouldn’t be an issue for you.

## How to Remove Coconut Oil Stains:

Okay, so you’re wondering how to remove coconut oil from clothes. If you’re short on time and need to get your laundry done immediately, we can help! Type in your [zip code here](https://wedeliverlaundry.com/sb/signup/) and allow us to take care of all your laundry needs.

Follow these 6 steps to help remove those stains.

1.  **Clean the coconut oil off**

It’s time to take action and clean off the excess oil as soon as you can. We recommend that you start off by grabbing either a towel, hand towel, or paper towel to clean the oil.

Next, grab the dishwashing liquid to use to clean off the coconut oil.

You’ll want to use the towel of your choice along with the dishwashing liquid of your choice to start gently scraping off the oil from the fabric.

It’s important that you aren’t rubbing harshly or not keeping the oil contained. Keep a slow pace as you clean off the oil.

2.  **Apply Baking Soda**

Once you’re finished removing the excess coconut oil, the next thing to grab is baking soda. The amount of oil that was spilled will determine how much baking soda to apply to the fabric.

You can also use [arrowroot powder or cornstarch](https://www.boldsky.com/home-n-garden/improvement/2014/six-natural-ways-to-starch-clothes/articlecontent-pf41092-040455.html) as a substitute for baking soda.

We recommend that you sprinkle baking soda onto the area the oil was spilled on.  

3.  **Let the baking soda sit**

If the stains are small, we recommend only leaving it to sit for 15 minutes.

If the stains are bigger, we encourage you to let the baking soda sit for 30 minutes to an hour.

Once it begins to clump, you’ll know it’s time to take your next step in the cleaning process.

4.  **Remove Baking Soda**

As the clumps begin to form, you will need to start scrapping off the baking soda from the fabric.

We suggest that you use a thin item like a credit card or a small piece of cardboard to gently scrape off the clumps of baking soda.

Make sure to thoroughly remove the baking soda from the fabric before jumping into the next step!

5.  **Wash it in hot water**

Throw the fabric affected by the coconut oil into the washer and use hot water instead of cold water.

It’s important that you wash it at the hottest setting to do your best to remove the coconut oil from the clothing.

6.  **Dry your clothes**

Pause on throwing it into the dryer.

Instead, air dry the items for the best results.
