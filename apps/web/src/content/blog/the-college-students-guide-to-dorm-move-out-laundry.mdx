Procrastination is the enemy during move-out week. Begin sorting your laundry at least a week in advance. Categorize clothes into whites, darks, delicates, and items that need special care. ### Understand Dorm Laundry Etiquette:

If you choose to do your laundry on campus, be mindful of shared facilities. Always remove your clothes promptly to avoid inconveniencing others. Clean the lint traps after using dryers and report any malfunctioning machines to campus maintenance. Respecting communal spaces ensures a smooth experience for everyone.

### Pack Smart: Clean Clothes Only:

Packing dirty clothes can lead to unpleasant odors and potential mold growth, especially if items remain in boxes for extended periods. Ensure all clothing is clean and thoroughly dried before packing. Using vacuum-sealed bags can save space and protect garments during transit.

### Invest in Quality Laundry Bags:

Durable laundry bags or hampers are essential for transporting clothes. Look for options with sturdy handles and ample capacity. Some bags even double as backpacks, making it easier to carry loads across campus or to your car.

### Consider Seasonal Storage Solutions:

For items not needed during the summer, consider storage solutions that offer laundry services. Some companies provide packages where they clean and store your clothes, delivering them fresh at the start of the next semester. This option is especially beneficial for students traveling long distances.
