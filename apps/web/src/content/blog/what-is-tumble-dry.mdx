# What is Tumble Dry?

So you’re wondering, “what does tumble dry even mean?” We’ve got you covered. 

When it comes to laundry, there are many settings and rules to follow, but we wanted to quickly unpack this term that everyone who does laundry must know. 

Tumble dry is when you put your laundry items into the dryer instead of air-drying them. Every setting you choose when using your dryer is considered a tumble dry setting. Pretty simple, right? 

While you may be used to leaving your clothes out to dry, choosing to tumble dry them is an efficient way to get your laundry piles marked off your to-do list. 

I mean, who doesn’t enjoy having warm and wrinkle-free clothes after drying them?

## What are Common Tumble Dryer Settings?

### What is Tumble Dry Low?

This setting is commonly known as the delicate/gentle option. It’s a low temperature that’s safest for delicate clothes, or items like wool and nylon. Keep in mind, this setting will increase your drying time.

Use this setting for:

● Delicate clothes  
● Wool  
● Nylon  
● Spandex

Be sure to check the care label before choosing this!

### What is Tumble Dry High?

If you’re drying cotton or have items that usually take longer to dry, then this is the best option for you.

Had a summer beach day with towels and swimsuits? We recommend using tumble dry high. Need those hoodies and jeans dried for the winter? Tumble dry high setting is the answer.

Use this setting for:

● Towels  
● Swimsuits  
● Hoodies  
● Jeans  
● Cotton items

## 3 Quick Tips for Tumble Drying:

1.  Check the [care labels](https://www.coats.com/en/Information-Hub/Care-Labels) and follow instructions
2.  Shake out items from the washer before using tumble dry setting
3.  Use a dryer sheet
