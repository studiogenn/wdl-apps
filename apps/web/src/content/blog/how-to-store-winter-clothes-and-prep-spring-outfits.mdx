As the temperature rises and days grow longer, it’s time to swap bulky winter layers for light, breathable spring attire. But before you push your winter wardrobe into the back of the closet, it’s essential to store your clothes properly and prep for the new season. Not only does this prolong the life of your garments, but it also ensures you’re stepping into spring with a fresh, clean start.

Whether you’re tackling laundry at home or looking for a laundry service near you, we’ve got expert tips to help you transition your wardrobe smoothly. Let’s dive into how to store winter clothes the right way and get your spring outfits ready for action.

## Why Seasonal Clothing Care Matters

Taking care of your seasonal wardrobe isn’t just about staying organized—it’s about protecting your investment in clothing. Wool sweaters, down coats, and thermal wear can degrade quickly if stored improperly, and spring clothing left unwashed from last year may harbor musty smells or even bacteria.

### Step 1: Clean Winter Clothes Thoroughly Before Storing

Before anything goes into storage, launder every piece of winter clothing. Dirt, oils, and even invisible stains like sweat or deodorant residue can settle in over time and attract pests like moths.

#### Best Practices:

-   Use a professional laundry service for delicate items like wool or cashmere.
-   Choose dry cleaning for down jackets or structured coats that can’t be washed.
-   Wash and completely dry everything to avoid mildew.
-   Pro Tip: Our wash and fold laundry service ensures your items are clean, fresh, and ready for storage—saving you hours of work.

### Step 2: Use the Right Storage Methods

Once everything is clean, the next step is to store your winter clothing in a way that keeps it protected until next season.

#### Key Tips:

-   Fold bulky items like sweaters and stack them with tissue paper to prevent creases.
-   Store in breathable cotton bags or bins with lids—avoid plastic, which traps moisture.
-   Add cedar blocks or lavender sachets to repel moths and keep things smelling fresh.
-   Label bins clearly for easy retrieval next season.

By storing clean and folded winter clothes properly, you’ll reduce wear and tear and keep everything fresh for next year.

### Step 3: Take Inventory of Spring Clothing

Before diving into spring shopping, pull out your warm-weather clothes and see what you already own. Chances are, some items will need a little TLC.

#### Do This:

-   Inspect for stains, holes, or fading.
-   Refresh pieces with a light wash or send them out to a laundry delivery service for a deep clean.
-   Donate anything that no longer fits or suits your style.

Not sure what’s clean and what’s not? Our pickup and delivery laundry service makes it easy to get your spring wardrobe freshened up without the hassle.

### Step 4: Freshen Up and Organize Your Closet

With winter clothing stored and spring clothes refreshed, it’s time to revamp your closet.

#### Closet Tips:

-   Switch to lighter hangers for spring garments.
-   Keep everyday items at eye level and formal wear or seasonal pieces higher up.
-   Doing a little closet organization now can save time each morning—and help you enjoy your wardrobe more.
