## Why Your Laundry Routine Matters

You may not realize it, but the way you wash your clothes can have a significant environmental impact. According to the EPA, the average American household does **300 loads of laundry per year**. That adds up to:

-   **6,000+ gallons of water**
-   High levels of **energy usage** (especially from hot water cycles and dryers)
-   Detergents and microplastics released into waterways

By shifting to _eco-friendly laundry habits_, you can minimize waste, save energy, and help protect our planet—without compromising on cleanliness.

### 1\. Wash with Cold Water

Nearly **90% of the energy** used in a washing machine goes toward heating the water. Cold water washes are just as effective for most fabrics and stains, and they help extend the life of your clothes while saving energy.  
✅ _Tip: Choose detergents that are formulated for cold water performance._

### 2\. Choose Green Detergents

Many mainstream detergents contain phosphates, synthetic fragrances, and non-biodegradable chemicals. Opt for plant-based, biodegradable options that come in **recyclable or refillable packaging.**  
💡 _Look for third-party certifications like EPA Safer Choice, USDA BioPreferred, or Leaping Bunny._

### 3\. Skip the Dryer When Possible

Air-drying your clothes not only reduces your carbon emissions but also **prolongs garment life**. Use a drying rack or a clothesline when weather permits.  
🌬️ _Bonus: Sunlight is a natural disinfectant and whitener!_

### 4\. Do Full Loads (but Don’t Overload)

Running full loads maximizes efficiency, but be careful not to overstuff the machine. Overloading prevents proper cleaning and rinsing, which may mean you need to rewash clothes—doubling your energy and water usage.

### 5\. Upgrade Your Appliances (or Outsource the Problem)

If your washer and dryer are over 10 years old, they likely use significantly more water and energy than ENERGY STAR® certified models. However, not everyone has the time, money, or space for an appliance upgrade.

### 7\. Ditch the Plastic Bags

If you’re still using plastic bags to carry your laundry or dry cleaning, it’s time for a change. Reusable laundry totes, mesh bags, or compostable garment covers are better for the environment and your style.

### 8\. Detox Your Closet

Green laundry habits don’t stop at the washing machine. Spring is a great time to **purge synthetic fabrics**, donate unused clothes, and embrace **slow fashion**. Fewer, higher-quality garments = fewer loads and longer garment life.  
♻️ _Tip: Consider joining local clothing swaps or textile recycling programs._
